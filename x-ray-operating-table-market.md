Mechanical tables use manual mechanisms such as cranks or levers to adjust the height, tilt, and other positions of the table. These tables are generally cost-effective and simple to maintain. On the other hand, electric tables are powered by electricity and offer more precise and convenient positioning options, often with remote control capabilities. These tables are preferred in settings where accuracy and ease of use are crucial, but they usually come at a higher cost.</p></p>
