<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The biodegradable nonwovens market can be categorized into three types: drylaid nonwovens, wetlaid nonwovens, and spunbond nonwovens. Drylaid nonwovens are made by entangling fibers using mechanical, thermal, or chemical processes. Wetlaid nonwovens are produced by mixing fibers in water and forming a web through a filtration process. Spunbond nonwovens are manufactured by extruding molten polymer filaments onto a conveyor belt, which are then bonded to create a fabric. All three types offer biodegradability, making them environmentally friendly alternatives in various applications such as textiles, hygiene products, and packaging.</p></p>
