<p><p>Dust suppressant market types refer to different categories of products used to control and reduce dust particles in various applications. </p><p>1. Hygroscopic salts: These salts absorb moisture from the air, preventing dust particles from becoming airborne.</p><p>2. Lignosulfonates: Derived from wood pulp, lignosulfonates bind loose particles together, reducing dust.</p><p>3. Petroleum resins: These resins are sticky substances that bind dust particles together, preventing them from becoming airborne.</p><p>4. Polymer emulsions: These emulsions form a thin film over dust particles, preventing them from becoming airborne.</p><p>5. Tar and bitumen emulsion products: These products contain sticky substances that trap dust particles, reducing their mobility.</p><p>6. Other market: This category includes various innovative or less common dust suppressant products not mentioned explicitly above.</p></p>
